              theory: '**Image as numpy array.** Every image OpenCV reads is `np.ndarray` of dtype `uint8`, shape `(H, W, 3)` for colour or `(H, W)` for grayscale. This means everything numpy supports (slicing, masking, broadcasting, vectorised arithmetic) just works on images. `img[100:200, 50:150]` crops a rectangle. `img[..., 0]` extracts the blue channel. `img.mean(axis=(0,1))` gives per-channel average colour.\n\n**Colour spaces beyond RGB.** OpenCV supports 30+ colour spaces; the ones you actually use:\n• **BGR** — default for `imread`. 3 channels, each 0-255.\n• **RGB** — what every other library expects. Use `cv2.COLOR_BGR2RGB`.\n• **Grayscale** — single channel; convert with `cv2.COLOR_BGR2GRAY`. Critical for edge detection, thresholding, classical features.\n• **HSV** (Hue-Saturation-Value) — best for colour-based segmentation. Hue is 0-179 in OpenCV (NOT 0-360!), Saturation and Value are 0-255. To detect "red things", filter by hue range 0-10 OR 170-179.\n• **LAB** — perceptually uniform; used in colour transfer, white-balance, and similarity comparisons.\n\n**Geometric transforms.** `cv2.resize(img, (w, h), interpolation=...)` — use `INTER_AREA` for shrinking, `INTER_CUBIC` or `INTER_LANCZOS4` for enlarging. `cv2.warpAffine` and `cv2.warpPerspective` for rotation, translation, shearing, perspective correction.\n\n**dtype gotcha.** uint8 wraps modulo 256. `img + img` overflows: `200 + 100 = 44`, not 300. For arithmetic, either cast to `int32`/`float32` first, or use `cv2.add` and `cv2.subtract` which **saturate** (clip to 0-255).\n\n**Video I/O.** `cv2.VideoCapture(0)` opens the webcam; `cap.read()` returns `(ret, frame)`. `cv2.VideoWriter` writes frames to a file with a specified codec (FOURCC) and FPS. Always `cap.release()` and `cv2.destroyAllWindows()` at the end or your camera stays locked.\n\n**Drawing primitives.** `cv2.rectangle`, `cv2.circle`, `cv2.line`, `cv2.putText`, `cv2.polylines`.
