              theory: '**The output tensor**: for an `s × s` grid, each cell predicts (anchor-free YOLOv8) one box: `[x, y, w, h, obj_conf, class_logits[K]]`. The full output is a tensor of shape `(s² × (5 + K))`. Multiple "scale" outputs are predicted at different resolutions to catch small/medium/large objects.\n\n**Loss** has three components:\n• **Box regression**: predict box coordinates accurately. Modern YOLO uses **CIoU** or **DIoU** loss instead of plain MSE.\n• **Object confidence**: predict whether a box contains ANY object.\n• **Classification**: cross-entropy over class labels.\n\n**Non-Maximum Suppression (NMS)**: post-process. For each class:\n1. Sort all predicted boxes by confidence.\n2. Keep the highest-confidence box.\n3. Remove every other box whose IoU with the kept box > `iou_thresh` (default 0.45).\n4. Repeat until no boxes left.\n\n**IoU = intersection-area / union-area**. Ranges 0 (no overlap) to 1 (perfect overlap).\n\n**Evaluation metric: mAP@IoU**:\n• For a fixed IoU threshold (typically 0.5), compute Precision and Recall at every confidence threshold.\n• Plot Precision vs Recall curve, take the area under the curve = **AP** for that class.\n• Average AP across all classes = **mAP**.\n• `mAP@0.5` is the classic Pascal VOC metric. `mAP@0.5:0.95` (averaged over IoU thresholds 0.5, 0.55, …, 0.95) is the COCO standard — much harder.\n\n**YOLOv8 model sizes** (Ultralytics):\n| Variant | Params | mAP@0.5 (COCO) | Speed (CPU) | Use case |\n|---|---|---|---|---|\n| `yolov8n` (nano) | 3.2M | 37 | 80ms | Edge / mobile / Jetson |\n| `yolov8s` (small) | 11.2M | 45 | 130ms | General laptop |\n| `yolov8m` (medium) | 25.9M | 50 | 230ms | Server, more accurate |\n| `yolov8l` (large) | 43.7M | 53 | 380ms | High accuracy |\n| `yolov8x` (xlarge) | 68.2M | 54 | 600ms | Best mAP, slowest |\n\n**YOLO label format** (`.txt` per image, one row per object):\n```\nclass_id  x_centre  y_centre  width  height\n```\nAll values **normalised to [0, 1]** by image width/height.
